# Argentina Packing List — Feb/Mar 2026

**Constraint**: Carry-on only. KLM international + JetSmart/Aerolíneas Argentinas internal flights.
⚠️ Check JetSmart carry-on limits — budget carriers often stricter (may be ~10kg).

**Climate**: Late summer. Buenos Aires hot (25–30°C). Patagonia variable (5–20°C, strong wind, possible rain). Bariloche moderate (10–22°C).

## Hiking & Outdoor
- [ ] Hiking boots (worn on flights to save bag space)
- [ ] Windbreaker
- [ ] Lightweight rain jacket (if windbreaker isn't waterproof)
- [ ] 1 pair quick-dry hiking pants
- [ ] 2–3 moisture-wicking shirts/base layers
- [ ] Warm fleece or mid-layer
- [ ] Hiking socks × 3
- [ ] Lightweight daypack (~20L, packable)
- [ ] Sunglasses
- [ ] Sunscreen (high SPF — Patagonia sun is strong)
- [ ] Hat/cap
- [ ] Buff/neck gaiter (wind protection in Patagonia)
- [ ] Trekking poles: **rent locally** in Calafate, Chalten, and Bariloche

## City / Casual
- [ ] 2–3 t-shirts
- [ ] 1 pair shorts (Buenos Aires heat)
- [ ] 1 pair casual pants or jeans
- [ ] Sneakers or casual shoes
- [ ] Light evening layer (BA can cool at night)

## Essentials
- [ ] Passport
- [ ] Phone + charger
- [ ] Power adapter (Argentina uses Type C and Type I — Type C same as NL, but bring a Type I adapter or universal)
- [ ] Travel insurance docs
- [ ] Reusable water bottle (1L+ for hike days)
- [ ] Headlamp or small flashlight (early starts on hikes)
- [ ] Basic first aid: blister plasters, ibuprofen, anti-diarrhea
- [ ] Toiletries (travel-size, ≤100ml for carry-on)

## Nice to Have
- [ ] Earplugs + eye mask (long flights, hostels)
- [ ] Dry bag or ziplock bags (rain protection for phone/docs on hikes)
- [ ] Portable power bank
- [ ] Kindle/book for flights and downtime
- [ ] Swimwear (Bariloche lakes, maybe Buenos Aires pool)

## NOT Packing (rent/buy locally)
- Trekking poles — rent at each hiking destination
- Heavy winter gear — not needed in late summer
